The Journey

Sep 20–23
3 nights
Algodon Mansion
Buenos Aires · Recoleta
  • Arrive into the city and let it come to you — no sightseeing agenda
  • Recoleta cemetery walk in early morning before the city wakes
  • Palacio Duhau gardens: one of the finest private gardens in BA
  • Librería El Ateneo — the great bookshop inside a 1919 theatre
  • Café afternoons in Recoleta, wine bars in Palermo after dark
  • Late dinners: Buenos Aires eats at 9 pm — lean into it
Buenos Aires is slow urban immersion, not city tourism. The Mansion's Recoleta address puts you in exactly the right neighborhood for this pace: architecture, culture, excellent wine, unhurried afternoons.
Sep 23–27
4 nights
★ Birthday — Night 2 · September 25th
Estancia Los Potreros
Córdoba Sierras · Argentina
  • Fly Buenos Aires → Córdoba, private transfer to the estancia in the sierras
  • Hosted warmth: this is a family-run estancia with real Argentine countryside culture
  • Walks through the sierras, long lunches, pool and veranda time, fireplace evenings
  • Food and wine at the heart of every day — Argentine parrilla, local Malbec
  • Horseback riding available, entirely optional — texture rather than identity
  • September 25: the birthday itself — see The Day tab
Los Potreros is intimate and human-scale — the emotional centre of the trip. Four nights here are right: enough to settle into the rhythm of the place and let the birthday evening feel earned.
Sep 27–Oct 1
4 nights
Casa de Uco
Mendoza · Uco Valley Vineyards
  • Fly Córdoba → Mendoza: Andes visible immediately on descent
  • Post-birthday restoration: spa, vineyard stillness, mountain light
  • One major experience per day maximum — a tasting menu, a blending session, a gourmet vineyard picnic or asado
  • The Relax Experience spa program: deeply aligned with the pacing of this moment in the trip
  • Cycling between vineyards at your own pace, sunset wine, long lunches that slide into the afternoon
  • The Andes close and clear every morning — this is the view you earn by slowing down
Mendoza as post-birthday afterglow is the strongest possible use of Casa de Uco. The spa, wine, and sensory richness here are designed precisely for this kind of restoration — not for checking things off, but for settling in.
Oct 1–3
2 nights
Buenos Aires — Return
Buenos Aires · Final nights
  • Fly Mendoza → Buenos Aires: familiar city now feels different after two weeks in the country
  • One full day with no obligations — let the trip exist before it ends
  • Long lunch at whichever restaurant the first three days missed
  • Final walk through Recoleta or Palermo at dusk
  • International departure October 3rd from EZE
A graceful re-entry rather than an abrupt ending. The city buffer allows the trip to close on its own terms, without the compression of airport stress after something this carefully built.

The Day

25 September 2026  ·  Estancia Los Potreros  ·  Córdoba Sierras

Night 2 at the Estancia

Already settled. Already at home. The day begins without needing to go anywhere.

Morning
Slow Breakfast at the Estancia
No alarm. The sierras outside the window, the air cooler than you expect. Breakfast at the estancia table — eggs, bread, fruit, strong coffee. No itinerary. No clock. This is how the day begins.
Late morning
Optional: Walk, Ride, or Waterfall
If the morning calls for it: a guided walk into the sierras, a slow horseback ride across the property, or the short excursion to a local waterfall through native landscape. If it doesn't — stay. Read. Sit on the veranda. The estancia is equally perfect in stillness.
Nothing on this day should feel like an obligation. The staff will offer; say yes or no with equal ease. The property is as beautiful when you do nothing in it.
Midday
Long Countryside Lunch
The centrepiece of the Argentine day. A proper lunch at the estancia — parrilla if available, always good wine, conversation, and no particular reason to leave the table. This is what the gaucho tradition of hospitality actually feels like when it's right.
Afternoon
Pool, Reading, Bath, Rest
The full Argentine afternoon. The pool in the sierra sun, a book that has been waiting, a long bath drawn slowly, rest that is genuinely restful. This is not filler — it is the architecture of the evening. The better the afternoon, the better what follows.
Sunset
Gin & Tonic Overlooking the Sierras
The Córdoba sierras at dusk are not subtle. The light goes gold, then amber, then a particular rose that you will not find at lower altitude. A gin and tonic — or champagne — already poured. The two of you, exactly here, on September 25th. Nothing needs to be said. The landscape says it.
Pre-arrange with the estancia: drinks set up at the viewpoint or terrace before sunset. A simple thing, perfectly placed.
Evening
Intimate Candlelit Dinner — Estancia Table
The fireplace. Candles. The particular warmth of a room that is being used for something it was built for. An exceptional Malbec from a Mendoza they will know well. Estancia-style cuisine — the food of a place that takes its ingredients seriously. The dinner is intimate and not performative. No announcement. No ceremony beyond what the evening itself provides.
Arrange with the estancia team well in advance: private dinner setting, wine selection from Mendoza producers, a birthday dessert if desired — kept understated. The staff here understand what this kind of evening requires.
Late
Fireplace. Conversation. The Rest of the Night.
The sierras are completely dark. The fire burns low. You have two more nights here — and then Mendoza, and then the city, and then home. Tonight is unhurried. Fifty arrived, and it arrived well.

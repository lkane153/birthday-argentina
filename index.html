Itinerary — 11 nights · September 2026

Nights 1–3 Algodon Mansion — Buenos Aires Sep 14–17

A sophisticated arrival into one of South America's great cities. The Mansion sits in Palermo, steps from Recoleta — the kind of neighborhood built for walking slowly, with its ornate architecture, shaded streets, and the unhurried rhythm of a city that comes alive after dark. Mornings for wandering, evenings for long meals and excellent wine.

Architecture walks Recoleta Cemetery Wine bars Late dinners Palermo streets No agenda
Nights 4–7 Casa de Uco — Mendoza Sep 17–21

Vineyard living at its most unhurried. The Andes close and clear on the horizon, rows of vines extending in every direction, and a lodge designed for lingering. One winery tasting per day, no more — this is not a tour, it's a settling in. Long lunches with afternoon light falling across the table. Spa. The specific quality of mountain silence at sunset with a glass already poured.

Vineyard walks One tasting/day max Spa Long lunches Andean light Sunset wine
Nights 8–11 Estancia Los Potreros — Córdoba Sep 21–25

A working estancia in the Córdoba sierras, intimate and lodge-based. Fireplace evenings, horseback rides across open land, long scenic drives through rolling hills. No spa agenda, no excursion schedule — just the deep quiet of a place far from everything, and the particular pleasure of arriving somewhere that asks nothing of you. Night 2 here is September 25th.
